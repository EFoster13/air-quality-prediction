# City Air Quality Profiles

## Los Angeles, CA
**Coordinates:** 34.0522, -118.2437

**Key Facts:**
- Population: ~4 million (metro: ~13 million)
- Geography: Coastal basin surrounded by mountains (traps pollution)
- Climate: Mediterranean (hot, dry summers)

**Pollution Characteristics:**
- Typical AQI: 50-100 (moderate)
- Peak seasons: Summer/Fall (heat + wildfires)
- Main sources: Vehicle emissions (largest contributor), port activity, refineries
- Historical context: Famous for smog in 1970s-80s, improved significantly but still struggles

**Recent Events:**
- 2024 wildfires caused multi-day hazardous AQI (200+)
- Traffic patterns: Worst during rush hours (7-9am, 4-7pm)

**What to watch:** PM2.5 (vehicles), O3 (sunlight + emissions), wildfire season

## Phoenix, AZ

**Key Facts:**

- Geography: Valley surrounded by mountains
- Climate: Hot desert (extreme summer heat)

**Pollution Characteristics:**
- Typical AQI: 40-90 (good to moderate)
- Peak seasons: Summer (O3 from heat) and dust storm season

## Madison, WI
**Coordinates:** 43.0731, -89.4012

**Key Facts:**
- Population: ~270,000
- Geography: Isthmus between two lakes
- Climate: Continental (cold winters, warm summers)

**Pollution Characteristics:**
- Typical AQI: 20-60 (good, occasionally moderate)
- Peak seasons: Summer (higher O3), winter (inversions trap pollution)
- Main sources: Vehicle traffic, residential heating, distant industrial
- Generally clean air compared to major metros

**Recent Events:**
- Canadian wildfire smoke in summer 2023/2024 caused temporary spikes
- Winter inversions can trap local emissions

**What to watch:** Seasonal variation, Canadian wildfire smoke transport, winter inversions
